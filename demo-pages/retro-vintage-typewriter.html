Country · Recorded 1962 · Doolin Ranch Sessions

Sawdust & Kerosene

Written and performed by Hank Doolin — transcribed by R. Whitmore, 1978
Verse 1

The barroom floor is sawdust-thick and twenty years of boots

Have worn a path from the door to the bar to the broken jukebox

I've been standing at the end of that path since my daddy brought me in

Said, son, this is the family church, and it smells of our next of kin

Chorus

Sawdust and kerosene, that's all this life has been

Splinter in your thumb and a lamp that burns too clean

But I'd take every splinter, every blister, every long-gone dream

For one more night of sawdust and kerosene

Verse 2

Old Earl played the fiddle till his bow arm gave him up

He still sits in the corner with his beer coffee cup

Nods his head in three-four time to whatever's on the box

A man whose music lives inside him like a watch that never stops

Chorus

Sawdust and kerosene, that's all this life has been

Splinter in your thumb and a lamp that burns too clean

But I'd take every splinter, every blister, every long-gone dream

For one more night of sawdust and kerosene

Verse 3

My wife says the place is a fire waiting to happen

She's right about that, like she's right about most things

But some fires are worth sitting next to till they catch

And some things in life only glow when they're about to go

Verse 4

They're putting a shopping mall out on Route 9

They'll take the land this bar sits on by the end of next summer

I already feel the cold from the parking lot

Already smell the concrete where the sawdust used to not

Final Chorus

Sawdust and kerosene, that's all this life has been

Splinter in your thumb and a lamp that burns too clean

Lord, I'd take every splinter, every blister, every long-gone dream

For one more night, just one more night

One more night of sawdust and kerosene

* Note in margin from original notebook: "change last verse — too obvious?" — H.D.
