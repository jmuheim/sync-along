Ashen Pale · 2023 · "The Cartographer's Last Winter"

November Cartography

Ashen Pale
I.

I have drawn every road that leads nowhere warm

Surveyed each valley that was not worth surveying

The compass needle rusts in November

It points, as it always has, toward what is leaving

Refrain

These are the maps of forgetting

Paper worn thin at the folds

Drawn in the ink of November

By hands that are growing too cold

II.

The village I charted no longer appears

In the census, the archive, the memory of stone

I left a blank space in the legend for it

The way you leave silence for what can't be known

III.

There is a cartographer's term — terra incognita

Unknown land. We fill it with imagined creatures

I have filled mine with the shape of your absence

A blank expanse that occupies the features

Refrain

These are the maps of forgetting

Paper worn thin at the folds

Drawn in the ink of November

By hands that are growing too cold

Bridge

Roll them up. Tie them with string.

Put them in the drawer beneath the window

The one that sticks in summer

The one that rattles all November long

IV.

When they find these after I am gone

They'll say: he mapped what couldn't be mapped

He spent his winters tracing roads to nothing

They'll burn them for warmth. That seems about right.

Final Refrain

These are the maps of forgetting

Paper worn thin at the folds

Drawn in the ink of November

By hands that have finally gone cold
