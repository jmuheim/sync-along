The Long Road Down from Calloway

Finn Ashgrove

A ballad in nine verses tracing a drovers' route through the highlands. Traditionally sung unaccompanied; Ashgrove's arrangement adds low whistle and frame drum from verse four.

Verse I

Before the frost had left the ground I shouldered up my pack

And took the old drover's road that nobody takes back

The birch trees stood like gateposts there on either side the hill

And everything was quiet and everything was still

Refrain

The long road down from Calloway, the long road into light

I walked it in the morning and I'll walk it through the night

Verse II

I passed the ruined sheiling where my grandfather was born

The roof had fallen inward and the doorframe had been torn

A thistle grew up through the floor, defiant in the damp

And something in its standing made me walk a little straight

Verse III

At Calloway the market's gone, they've turned it to a car park

The cattle pens are tarmac now, the weighbridge just a mark

An old man sat on what remained of someone's garden wall

He didn't look at me at all, just watched the drizzle fall

Refrain

The long road down from Calloway, the long road into light

I walked it in the morning and I'll walk it through the night

Verse IV

Below the ridge at Drumochy where the river takes a bend

There's a cairn of stones so ancient nobody knows when

I added to it, as you do, a stone from my coat pocket

And stood a while in silence like the land itself could clock it

Verse V

The rain came on at Ashfield cross and soaked me to the skin

I found a barn and made my bed in hay that smelled of sin

Or maybe just of winter and of animals and soil

The kind of smell that cities spend a thousand years to spoil

Refrain

The long road down from Calloway, the long road into light

I walked it in the morning and I'll walk it through the night

Verse VI

A woman at a farmhouse gave me oatcake and a dram

She asked me where I'd come from and I told her what I am

A man with no particular direction or desire

She laughed at that and pointed me toward the kitchen fire

Verse VII

Her father had made this same walk in nineteen fifty-three

She showed me photographs of him, as young as I could be

He wore the same expression, that half-grin, half-surprise

Of someone who has just walked out from under their own skies

Refrain

The long road down from Calloway, the long road into light

I walked it in the morning and I'll walk it through the night

Verse VIII

I crossed the final moorland just as evening turned to blue

The kind of blue that has no name in any tongue I knew

The road went straight as memory down into the glen

And I thought of all the people who will walk this road again

Verse IX

And so I came to the valley town with mud upon my boots

No richer than I'd started out, no clearer on the roots

But something in the walking had untangled what was tied

The long road down from Calloway had sorted out my stride

Final Refrain

The long road down from Calloway, the long road into light

I walked it in the morning and I'll walk it through the night

The long road down from Calloway, I'll not forget the way

A road that only finds you when you've nothing left to say

* Traditional melody, new words © Finn Ashgrove. May be freely sung at sessions.
